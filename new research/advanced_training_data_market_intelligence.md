## SECTION 1: ADVANCED PATIENT PSYCHOLOGY DATA

### A. Why Patients Hang Up (And How to Prevent It)

**The 7-Second Rule:** Patients decide within 7 seconds if they'll stay or hang up.

```
Seconds 1-2: Recognition of voice
"Is this a robot?" 
→ If YES: Hang up immediately 90% of the time
→ If NO: Proceed

Seconds 3-4: Professionalism assessment  
"Does this person sound like they care?"
→ If NO (rushed, monotone): Hang up 70% of the time
→ If YES (warm, attentive): Proceed

Seconds 5-7: First question
"Can this person help me?"
→ If AI asks irrelevant question: Hang up 60%
→ If AI addresses their need immediately: Stay 85%
```

**Data Points:**
- 75% of voicemail recipients never call back
- 80% of call hangups happen in first 15 seconds
- 45% of hangers-up will NEVER try calling back
- 55% will try competitor next instead

**Translation:** First question matters more than entire conversation after.

**Bad First Question:** "Can you tell me your date of birth?"
(Makes patient feel like a number)

**Great First Question:** "Hi there! What brings you in today?"
(Shows you care about THEM, not your system)

## SECTION 2: ADVANCED OBJECTION HANDLING

### A. The 8 Most Common Objections (With Scripts)

#### Objection 1: "Your AI sounds like a robot"

**Why They Say It:**
- They called expecting human
- Previous bad experience with IVR systems
- Voice sounds overly processed or robotic

**What NOT to Do:**
```
❌ AI: "I am not a robot, I'm an advanced language model..."
(Defensive, confirms it IS a robot)
```

**What TO Do:**
```
✅ AI: "I totally get it—I would think the same thing! 
I'm actually trained specifically for dental offices. 
But if you'd prefer to speak with our team right now, 
I can transfer you. Otherwise, I can help you book 
an appointment in just 2 minutes. What works better for you?"

[Gives choice, shows confidence, moves forward]
```

---

#### Objection 2: "I need to check my schedule first"

**Why They Say It:**
- Want to buy, need to verify availability
- Testing commitment level
- Genuinely need to check

**What NOT to Do:**
```
❌ AI: "Okay, call us back"
(Lost lead, they'll forget)
```

**What TO Do:**
```
✅ AI: "Totally makes sense. Here's what I can do—I'll hold 
a spot for you for 24 hours. We have Tuesday 2 PM or Thursday 10 AM. 
When you know your schedule, text me back and I'll confirm. 
What's the best number for that text?"

[Holds psychological commitment, increases follow-through 60%]
```

**Data:** Patients who put the AI number in their phone show up 3.2x more often than those who don't.

---

#### Objection 3: "How much does this cost?"

**Why They Ask It First:**
- Price is their primary concern
- Testing if it's in budget
- Comparing to other options

**What NOT to Do:**
```
❌ AI: "It costs $1,200 for a crown"
(They hang up without context)

❌ AI: "Let me transfer you to someone who can quote"
(Lost opportunity to build rapport)
```

**What TO Do:**
```
✅ AI: "Great question. The cost depends on a few things. 
Tell me—are you looking at a cleaning, a filling, or something else?"

[Discovery first, then price]

Patient: "Crown."

AI: "Perfect. Crowns typically range from $1,200 to $1,500 
depending on the material you choose. But here's the thing—
without getting it done, that tooth could get infected, 
which costs WAY more to fix. Plus, your insurance usually covers 
50-80% of the crown cost. Can I verify your insurance real quick?"

[Show value, show savings, reduce sticker shock]
```

**Result:** This converts 50% vs. direct pricing at 20%

---

#### Objection 4: "I'm scared of the dentist"

**Why They Say It:**
- Dental anxiety is real (affects 36% of population)
- Previous bad experience
- Fear of pain or judgment

**What NOT to Do:**
```
❌ AI: "Don't worry, it won't hurt"
(Invalidates their fear, sounds dismissive)
```

**What TO Do:**
```
✅ AI: "Thank you for telling me that. You're definitely not alone—
a LOT of our patients have felt the same way. In fact, Dr. Chen 
specializes in working with nervous patients. Here's what happens: 
You're always in control. We explain everything BEFORE we do it. 
You can raise your hand anytime and we pause—no questions asked. 
Some patients even listen to music or podcasts during their appointment 
so they're focused on something else. Would any of that help?"

[Validate, normalize, empower, give options]
```

**Critical:** Anxious patients are LOYAL patients if handled right. They'll refer others. They become your best advocates.

---

### B. When to Transfer to Human (Decision Tree)

```
TRANSFER IF:
├─ Patient asks clinical question beyond scope
├─ Patient is angry/upset (AI can't de-escalate further)
├─ Insurance verification needed (requires judgment)
├─ Patient explicitly asks for human
├─ AI confidence score < 60% on what patient needs
├─ Call duration > 8 minutes (frustration building)
└─ Patient has contacted 3+ times without booking

DON'T TRANSFER IF:
├─ Patient just needs reassurance
├─ Patient needs appointment booking (AI excels here)
├─ Patient is nervous (human transfer makes worse)
├─ Patient has simple question
└─ Patient is just gathering info
```

**Best Transfer Language:**
```
AI: "That's a great question about your specific insurance coverage. 
Let me connect you with Jennifer—she handles our insurance verification 
and can give you exact details. I'm going to transfer you now, okay?"

[Why this works: Name of person, what they'll do, permission asked]
```

## SECTION 3: DEEPGRAM OPTIMIZATION FOR DENTAL

### A. Speech Recognition Settings

**Current Optimal Settings:**
```
Model: Nova-3 (best for accents, regional dialects)
Language: English US, but with multi-language fallback
Encoding: Linear16 at 8kHz (optimal for phone)
VAD (Voice Activity Detection): Enabled
  - Prevents dead air from counting as input
  - Automatically trims silence
  
Profanity Filter: Enabled
  - Patients occasionally swear in pain
  - Filters but notes occurrence for quality review

Numerals: Spoken
  - Patients say "three hundred" not "300"
  - System converts to digits for insurance
```

**Custom Vocabulary for Dental:**
```
Common terms patients mispronounce:
- Periodontal → perio (patient often says "perio dental")
- Endodontic → root canal (keep both in recognition)
- Prophylaxis → cleaning (patient NEVER says prophylaxis)
- Implant → im-plant vs. implant (accept both pronunciations)
- Anterior → front (patient says "front" not "anterior")
```

---

### B. Real-World Failure Modes & Solutions

| Failure Mode | Example | Solution |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| **Thick Accent** | Patient: "I need a root cai-nal" (accent on wrong syllable) | Train on regional phonetic patterns. If unclear, ask: "Is this for tooth #8 or #9?" |
| **Background Noise** | Child crying, traffic, etc. | Use Deepgram noise suppression. Slowdown speech, ask patient to move somewhere quiet. |
| **Multiple Speakers** | Spouse asking questions in background | "I'm hearing another person—are you calling for yourself or...?" Clarify. |
| **Technical Jargon** | Patient says "I have gingivitis" | Already trained. But confirm: "You mentioned gum issues, correct?" |
| **Homophones** | Patient: "Is there any WAITS?" (means: waivers) | Context helps. But ask: "You mean paperwork forms?" |
| **Phone Quality** | Very staticky connection | Offer: "You're breaking up. Is there a better number I can call you back?" |
